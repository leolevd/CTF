# PARTNERING AGREEMENT

**EFFECTIVE DATE:** January 15, 2024

**PARTIES INVOLVED:**
- **PARTNER A:** TechCore Solutions, Inc., a corporation duly organized and existing under the laws of Delaware, with principal offices at 1500 Innovation Drive, San Francisco, California 94105
- **PARTNER B:** Digital Ventures LLC, a limited liability company organized under the laws of Nevada, with principal offices at 2800 Corporate Plaza, Las Vegas, Nevada 89154

---

## 1. INTRODUCTION

1.1 This Partnering Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of the Effective Date set forth above, by and between Partner A and Partner B (individually a "Party" and collectively the "Parties").

1.2 The Parties desire to form a strategic partnership to collaborate, combine resources, and pursue joint development, commercialization, and distribution activities for certain technology, intellectual property, and business initiatives related to the products and services developed by TechCore Solutions, Inc.

1.3 This Agreement sets forth the terms and conditions under which the Parties will work together to advance their shared objectives, allocate responsibilities, manage risk, and preserve intellectual property rights.

1.4 The Parties acknowledge that a successful partnership requires deliberate governance, high-level strategic alignment, and a clear framework for communication, decision-making, and dispute resolution.

---

## 2. RECITALS

WHEREAS, Partner A is the owner of certain intellectual property rights, patents, trademarks, copyrights, trade secrets, source code, object code, algorithms, processes, and proprietary technology that are important to the Parties’ mutual goals; and

WHEREAS, Partner B possesses complementary market access, business development capabilities, and financial resources that will support the acceleration of the Parties’ joint initiatives; and

WHEREAS, Partner A and Partner B wish to establish a formal partnership framework to coordinate efforts, share expertise, and pursue opportunities arising from their combined technology portfolios and market reach; and

WHEREAS, the Parties intend to govern their joint activities in a manner that protects their respective interests while maximizing the value of their collective efforts;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, promises, and agreements contained in this Agreement and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

## 3. DEFINITIONS

For purposes of this Agreement, the following terms shall have the meanings set forth below:

3.1 **"Agreement"** means this Partnering Agreement together with all schedules, exhibits, appendices, and written amendments executed by the Parties.

3.2 **"Business Activities"** means the joint activities, operations, marketing, commercialization, distribution, and customer engagement efforts undertaken by the Parties in furtherance of the partnership.

3.3 **"Confidential Information"** means any and all information disclosed by one Party to the other Party, whether written, electronic, oral, or visual, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure.

3.4 **"Joint Assets"** means any technology, designs, inventions, developments, enhancements, marketing assets, customer data, and business processes created, developed, or otherwise arising directly from the joint activities of the Parties under this Agreement.

3.5 **"Licensed Technology"** means the technology, patents, copyrights, trade secrets, software, algorithms, and intellectual property described in Schedule 1, including versions 1.0 through 5.3 of the TechCore Enterprise Suite.

3.6 **"Net Revenues"** means the gross revenues actually received by the Partnership from the sale, licensing, or distribution of products, services, or solutions developed or marketed under this Agreement, less returns, refunds, credits, and customary deductions.

3.7 **"Partnering Committee"** means the governance body described in Section 12 of this Agreement, which is responsible for decision-making, strategic oversight, and resolution of partnership matters.

3.8 **"Regulatory Approvals"** means any licenses, permits, authorizations, certifications, approvals, or other regulatory clearances required for the conduct of the Business Activities in any jurisdiction.

3.9 **"Retained Materials"** means any materials, documentation, or intellectual property identified in Schedule 2 that a Party elects to retain for its own use and that are excluded from the joint obligations of the Partnership.

3.10 **"Term"** means the period during which this Agreement remains in effect, as defined in Section 19.

## 4. PURPOSE OF THE PARTNERSHIP

4.1 The primary purpose of the partnership is to collaborate on the development, commercialization, and distribution of advanced software solutions, enterprise services, and related technology offerings that leverage the Parties’ combined strengths.

4.2 The partnership is intended to drive innovation, accelerate go-to-market timelines, optimize product and service delivery, and establish a stronger position in key markets, including but not limited to enterprise software, cloud services, digital transformation, and emerging technology solutions.

4.3 The Parties agree to coordinate efforts across the following focus areas:

(a) joint research and development initiatives;
(b) joint marketing and branding programs;
(c) co-selling and co-marketing with existing and prospective customers;
(d) customer onboarding and support processes;
(e) strategic alliances with third-party providers; and
(f) shared operational infrastructure and systems where beneficial.

4.4 The Parties further agree that this partnership will respect and preserve their individual corporate identities while presenting a unified value proposition to customers and stakeholders.

---

## 5. SCOPE OF COLLABORATION

5.1 The Parties shall collaborate on the following categories of work:

(a) evaluation and refinement of the Licensed Technology portfolio;
(b) identification and prioritization of joint go-to-market opportunities;
(c) development of joint product roadmaps and release plans;
(d) integration of complementary services, technology stacks, and customer-facing solutions;
(e) alignment of sales channels, distribution partners, and business development activities;
(f) joint customer engagements, pilots, and proof-of-concept deployments.

5.2 Each Party shall contribute personnel, expertise, financial resources, and other assets in accordance with the contribution plan described in Schedule 3.

5.3 The Parties shall coordinate on program budgets, performance milestones, and resource allocations through the Partnering Committee.

5.4 The Parties agree that any expansion of the scope of collaboration beyond the categories listed in Section 5.1 shall be subject to a written amendment executed by both Parties.

---

## 6. CONTRIBUTIONS AND RESPONSIBILITIES

6.1 Partner A shall provide the following contributions to the partnership:

(a) access to the Licensed Technology and related documentation;
(b) technical leadership, product management, and innovation oversight;
(c) engineering support for integration, customization, and quality assurance;
(d) access to key technical personnel, subject to resource availability and business priorities;
(e) transition assistance, training, and knowledge transfer as described in Section 7.

6.2 Partner B shall provide the following contributions to the partnership:

(a) market intelligence, sales channels, and distribution support;
(b) business development, account management, and customer relationship management services;
(c) commercial finance capabilities, pricing support, and revenue optimization;
(d) access to strategic partners, channel ecosystems, and go-to-market networks;
(e) implementation support for customer deployments and ongoing support services.

6.3 Both Parties shall maintain responsibility for their own personnel, facilities, corporate overhead, and internal costs, except as otherwise agreed in writing by the Parties.

6.4 The Parties acknowledge that each Party retains sole responsibility for its own compliance with applicable laws, regulatory obligations, and corporate governance requirements.

6.5 The Parties shall assign a liaison representative to coordinate communications, exchange information, and resolve routine issues on an ongoing basis.

---

## 7. TRANSITION AND KNOWLEDGE TRANSFER

7.1 Partner A shall provide reasonable transition assistance to Partner B and the Partnership for a period of one hundred eighty (180) days following the Effective Date.

7.2 Transition assistance shall include:

(a) comprehensive training on the Licensed Technology and joint solution architecture;
(b) access to all documentation, technical manuals, specifications, and relevant source materials;
(c) joint workshops, seminars, and whiteboarding sessions for key stakeholders;
(d) consultation services for design, development, and deployment activities, not to exceed one thousand (1,000) hours at no additional cost beyond any payment obligations set forth in this Agreement;
(e) introduction to key technical personnel, customers, and ecosystem partners.

7.3 Partner A shall deliver all relevant documentation, source code, technical materials, and specifications relating to the Licensed Technology within fifteen (15) days of the Effective Date.

7.4 Each Party shall make reasonable efforts to preserve continuity of knowledge and minimize disruption to existing operations during the transition period.

---

## 8. FINANCIAL TERMS

8.1 The Parties agree that the financial structure of the partnership shall be established in accordance with the terms set forth in Schedule 4.

8.2 Partner B shall contribute an initial partnership funding amount of $2,500,000.00 in recognition of the value of the Licensed Technology and the joint business opportunity.

8.3 Additional funding commitments from Partner B shall be made as follows:

(a) $1,500,000.00 due within thirty (30) days of the Effective Date;
(b) $1,000,000.00 due within ninety (90) days of the Effective Date.

8.4 Partner A and Partner B agree to a royalty-sharing arrangement for Net Revenues derived from the Partnership’s joint activities:

(a) Partner A shall be entitled to a royalty equal to three percent (3%) of Net Revenues for a period of five (5) years following the Effective Date;
(b) royalty payments shall be calculated quarterly and remitted within thirty (30) days following the end of each calendar quarter.

8.5 Payment shall be made by wire transfer of immediately available funds to the account designated by the receiving Party.

8.6 Any payment not made when due shall accrue interest at a rate of one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is less, from the due date until paid.

8.7 Each Party shall keep accurate and complete financial records relating to the Partnership and shall permit the other Party to inspect such records upon reasonable notice.

## 9. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS

9.1 Ownership of Pre-Existing Intellectual Property.

(a) Each Party retains ownership of all intellectual property that it owned prior to the Effective Date, including technology, patents, copyrights, trade secrets, trademarks, and proprietary materials.
(b) Anything identified as Retained Materials in Schedule 2 shall remain the sole property of the retaining Party.

9.2 Joint Assets.

(a) Joint Assets created by the Parties in the course of the Business Activities shall be owned jointly by the Parties in proportion to their respective contributions, unless otherwise agreed in writing.
(b) The Parties shall define ownership percentages for such Joint Assets in Schedule 5 or by subsequent written agreement.

9.3 Licensed Technology.

(a) Partner A grants Partner B, for the Term of this Agreement, a non-exclusive, non-transferable, worldwide license to use the Licensed Technology in connection with the Business Activities.
(b) The license shall include the right to reproduce, modify, distribute, market, and support the Licensed Technology solely as necessary to fulfill the partnership objectives.

9.4 New Developments.

(a) Inventions, improvements, or enhancements developed solely by one Party in the context of the partnership shall be owned by that Party, subject to any joint ownership agreements or license rights granted herein.
(b) Inventions, improvements, or enhancements developed jointly by the Parties shall be jointly owned, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

9.5 Reservation of Rights.

(a) Except for the rights expressly granted in this Agreement, no rights or licenses are granted by either Party to the other.
(b) Each Party expressly reserves all rights not granted herein.

9.6 Protection of Intellectual Property.

(a) The Parties shall cooperate to protect Joint Assets and the Licensed Technology through appropriate registrations, filings, and enforcement actions.
(b) Costs associated with prosecution, maintenance, defense, and enforcement of jointly owned intellectual property shall be shared as agreed by the Parties in Schedule 6.

9.7 Third-Party Claims.

(a) Each Party shall notify the other promptly of any claim, demand, or action alleging infringement or misappropriation of intellectual property rights related to the partnership.
(b) The Parties shall cooperate in the defense of any such claim in accordance with Section 15.

## 10. CONFIDENTIALITY

10.1 Each Party agrees to maintain in strict confidence all Confidential Information received from the other Party in connection with this Agreement.

10.2 Confidential Information shall not be disclosed to any third party without the prior written consent of the disclosing Party, except as required by law, regulation, or court order.

10.3 The confidentiality obligations under this Section 10 shall survive for a period of five (5) years following the termination or expiration of this Agreement.

10.4 The Parties may disclose Confidential Information to their respective employees, contractors, advisors, and affiliates on a need-to-know basis, provided that such persons are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth in this Agreement.

10.5 The following information shall not be deemed Confidential Information:

(a) information that is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement;
(b) information already known to the receiving Party prior to disclosure, as evidenced by written records;
(c) information independently developed by the receiving Party without use of the disclosing Party’s Confidential Information;
(d) information obtained from a third party who has no obligation of confidentiality.

10.6 If a Party is compelled by law to disclose Confidential Information, that Party shall provide prompt written notice to the disclosing Party and cooperate in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy.

## 12. PARTNERING COMMITTEE AND GOVERNANCE

12.1 The Parties shall establish a Partnering Committee to oversee the governance of the partnership.

12.2 The Partnering Committee shall consist of representatives designated by each Party, with each Party entitled to equal representation.

12.3 The Partnering Committee shall have the authority and responsibility to:

(a) approve joint business plans, budgets, and resource allocations;
(b) review and approve joint product roadmaps and release schedules;
(c) monitor compliance with this Agreement;
(d) resolve material issues, disputes, and performance matters;
(e) evaluate the need for amendments to the partnership structure;
(f) review partnership performance metrics and key performance indicators.

12.4 Meetings of the Partnering Committee shall be held at least quarterly, with additional meetings as necessary.

12.5 Decisions of the Partnering Committee shall be made by consensus, provided that if the Committee cannot reach consensus on a material matter, the dispute resolution process set forth in Section 17 shall apply.

12.6 The Partnering Committee may establish subcommittees for specialized topics, including technology review, sales strategy, compliance, and risk management.

12.7 Each Party shall bear its own costs of participation in the Partnering Committee, unless otherwise agreed by the Parties.

## 15. INDEMNIFICATION

15.1 Each Party shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the other Party and its affiliates, officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any and all losses, liabilities, damages, claims, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys’ fees) arising out of or resulting from:

(a) any breach of this Agreement;
(b) any negligence, willful misconduct, or fraudulent act of the indemnifying Party;
(c) any third-party claim arising from the indemnifying Party’s acts or omissions in connection with the Business Activities;
(d) any claim that the indemnifying Party’s pre-existing intellectual property infringes a third party’s rights, except to the extent such claim arises from the use of jointly owned or Partner B-modified technology.

15.2 Partner A shall defend and indemnify Partner B for claims arising from:

(a) any breach of Partner A’s representations, warranties, or covenants;
(b) any infringement or misappropriation of third-party intellectual property rights by the Licensed Technology;
(c) any failure of the Licensed Technology to comply with applicable laws, regulations, or industry standards as of the Effective Date.

15.3 Partner B shall defend and indemnify Partner A for claims arising from:

(a) Partner B’s marketing, sales, or distribution activities;
(b) Partner B’s modifications or customizations of the Licensed Technology beyond the scope authorized in writing;
(c) Partner B’s breach of confidentiality obligations or misuse of Partner A’s Confidential Information.

15.4 The indemnified Party shall provide prompt written notice of any claim, allow the indemnifying Party the opportunity to assume defense of the claim, and cooperate in the defense and settlement of the claim.

15.5 The indemnifying Party shall not settle any claim in a manner that imposes liability or admission of wrongdoing on the indemnified Party without the indemnified Party’s prior written consent.

## 17. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

17.1 The Parties shall use their best efforts to resolve any dispute, controversy, or claim arising under or relating to this Agreement through good faith negotiations.

17.2 If the dispute cannot be resolved by negotiation within thirty (30) days following written notice, the Parties shall escalate the matter to senior executives of each Party for further discussion.

17.3 If the dispute remains unresolved after escalation, the Parties agree to submit the dispute to binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association in accordance with its Commercial Arbitration Rules.

17.4 The arbitration shall be conducted in San Francisco, California, unless the Parties agree otherwise in writing.

17.5 The arbitrator shall apply the substantive law of the State of California, without regard to its conflict of law provisions.

17.6 Each Party shall bear its own costs and expenses associated with the arbitration, unless otherwise determined by the arbitrator.

17.7 The decision of the arbitrator shall be final and binding on the Parties, and judgment upon the award may be entered in any court having jurisdiction.

## 18. TERM AND TERMINATION

18.1 Term.

(a) This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in effect for a period of five (5) years, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Agreement.
(b) The Term may be extended by mutual written agreement of the Parties.

18.2 Termination for Cause.

(a) Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice.
(b) If the breach cannot reasonably be cured within thirty (30) days, the breaching Party shall have an additional sixty (60) days to cure the breach, provided that it commences cure promptly and diligently pursues resolution.

18.3 Termination for Convenience.

(a) Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon ninety (90) days’ prior written notice to the other Party.
(b) Termination for convenience shall not affect the Parties’ respective rights and obligations with respect to activities or obligations incurred prior to the termination date.

18.4 Effect of Termination.

(a) Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, each Party shall return or destroy the other Party’s Confidential Information and any materials containing such information.
(b) The Parties shall wind down joint activities in an orderly manner and fulfill any outstanding obligations to customers and partners.
(c) License rights granted under this Agreement shall terminate upon expiration or termination, except for any rights expressly surviving termination under this Agreement.

18.5 Survival.

(a) The provisions of Sections 9, 10, 11, 15, 17, 18.4, 19, and any other provision that by its nature should survive termination or expiration shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement.
